My Inspiration: Ratan Tata

Early Life & Education (1937 - 1962)

Born into the Tata family, Ratan Naval Tata received his bachelor's degree in architecture from Cornell University in 1962. His upbringing instilled in him a deep sense of responsibility and dedication.

Joining Tata Group (1962)

He began his career in the Tata Group in 1962, working on the shop floor at Tata Steel. This hands-on experience gave him a foundational understanding of the company's operations.

Chairmanship (1991 - 2012)

Took over as Chairman of the Tata Group in 1991. Under his leadership, the group transformed into a global conglomerate, making significant acquisitions like Tetley, Corus, and Jaguar Land Rover.

Nano & Philanthropy

Conceptualized the Tata Nano, aiming to provide an affordable car for Indian families. Known for his humility and extensive philanthropic efforts, directing a large portion of Tata's profits to charitable trusts.

Post-Chairmanship (2012 - Present)

Remained active as Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons and continues to be an influential figure in business and philanthropy, investing in startups and advocating for ethical leadership.
